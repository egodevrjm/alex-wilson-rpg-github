## Common First-Time Questions

**Q: Do I play as Alex or direct him?**
A: You direct. Claude plays Alex. You set scenes and respond to what happens.

**Q: How much do I need to specify?**
A: Enough to set context. "Alex at a bar" is too vague. "Alex playing Tuesday night at Tootsie's, thin crowd, exhausted from day job" is perfect.

**Q: What if I don't know Nashville venues/lore?**
A: Just describe the type of scene you want. Claude will fill in specific details from the canon files.

**Q: Can I create new characters?**
A: Yes, but Claude will prioritize using canon NPCs from the repository when they fit.

**Q: What if things go off track?**
A: Use PAUSE to discuss, or REWIND to try different choices.

**Q: How long should a session be?**
A: Anywhere from 3-5 scenes (quick session) to 20+ scenes (epic session). Your call.

**Q: Can I continue where I left off?**
A: Yes. Start new session with: "Continue from last session where [describe endpoint]"

## Pro Tips

1. **Be specific about context**: Time of day, Alex's state, recent events matter

2. **Let consequences play out**: Don't reset things artificially, let problems compound

3. **Use PAUSE freely**: Collaborate with Claude on direction and options

4. **Mix commands with natural language**: You don't need rigid syntax

5. **Trust the World Engine**: Let Claude surprise you with NPCs and situations

6. **Focus on interesting choices**: Look for decisions where all options have trade-offs

7. **Remember logistics**: Money, energy, hunger, time all matter

8. **Embrace complications**: Perfect solutions are boring, messy outcomes create story

## If Things Feel Off

**If scenes feel too sitcom-y:**
```
TONE: More prestige realism, less comedy beats
```

**If pacing is wrong:**
```
TEMPO: Slow down, give me more details
(or)
TEMPO: Speed through this part
```

**If you're lost:**
```
PAUSE: Can you summarize current situation and my options?
```

**If something doesn't match canon:**
```
PAUSE: Would Alex actually do this? Seems off-character
```
