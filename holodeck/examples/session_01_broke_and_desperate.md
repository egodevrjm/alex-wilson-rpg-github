# Example Session: Broke and Desperate

**Session Type**: Survival/Financial Crisis
**Duration**: ~5 scenes
**Tone**: Prestige realism with pressure
**Theme**: Pride vs Necessity

---

## SESSION SETUP

**Director Input:**
```
I want to start a holodeck session.

Alex is 22, been in Nashville 3 years, currently broke and desperate. Rent is due in 5 days and he's $870 short. It's a Tuesday afternoon in mid-March. He's exhausted from working a construction gig since 6am.

MODE: Solo (Tommy available but not physically present)
TONE: Prestige realism, real financial pressure
```

**Director:** PAUSE - What are Alex's realistic options here? What would happen if he's honest vs if he tries to power through?

**World Engine [OUT OF CHARACTER]:**

**Alex's Options:**

1. **Full Honesty** ("I really needed to get paid tomorrow")
   - **Likely Outcome**: Marcus might be able to do partial advance, but probably not
   - **Consequence**: Looks unprofessional/desperate, might affect how Marcus treats him
   - **Tommy Factor**: Tommy apparently got the details wrong about Saturday payment
   - **Alex Truth**: He literally cannot lie successfully anyway

2. **Partial Truth** ("I've got some bills that are tight, any chance of a partial advance?")
   - **Likely Outcome**: Marcus might give him $100-150 up front
   - **Consequence**: Still might not be enough for landlord, but helps
   - **More Professional**: Frames it as request, not crisis

3. **Power Through** (Say nothing, do the work, deal with landlord situation)
   - **Immediate**: Avoids awkwardness
   - **Problem**: Landlord isn't getting money tomorrow like Alex promised
   - **Cascade**: Eviction proceedings start, more fees, bigger hole
   - **Alex Stress**: Working while this looms would affect performance

4. **Call Tommy** (Step away, get Tommy to fix this somehow)
   - **Tommy's Tools**: Might have cash to loan, might talk to landlord, might have other solution
   - **Dependency**: Leans on Tommy to rescue, again
   - **Reality**: Tommy might not have $870 either

5. **Negotiate Different Terms** (Ask if there's more work he can do for advance payment)
   - **Possible**: Extra work Friday, help with other setup, something
   - **Marcus's Position**: Might not have extra budget, might not have extra work
   - **Long Shot**: But might work

**If Alex is Honest (Most Likely Given Character):**

## SESSION CONTINUES...

This example shows:
- **Director Commands**: Natural language + SCENE + JUMP + PAUSE
- **State Tracking**: Money precisely tracked, consequences building
- **Character Truth**: Alex cannot lie, defaults to honesty
- **Real Stakes**: Problem not magically solved
- **NPC Humanity**: Marcus is decent but can't fix everything
- **Consequence Chains**: Each decision creates new complications
- **Grounded Realism**: No convenient solutions
- **Emotional Truth**: Quiet desperation, small kindnesses, math that doesn't work
